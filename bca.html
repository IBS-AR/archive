BCA Workshop 2007: Multilevel Models

The Biostatistics Collaboration of Australia (BCA, www.bca.edu.au) and the Australasian Epidemiological Association (AEA, www.aea.asn.au) are pleased to announce a one day workshop in Mutlilevel Models in Hobart on Sunday 26th August 2007, immediately preceding the 2007 Joint Scientific Meetings of AEA/IEA-Western Pacific Region in Hobart, 26-29 August 2007.

The principal speaker will be Professor Alastair Leyland, who heads the program on Measuring Health, Variations in Health and the Determinants of Health at the MRC Social and Public Health Sciences Unit at the University of Glasgow, UK. His recent research has focussed on inequalities in mortality in Scotland and he is one of the principal investigators on the National Evaluation of Sure Start. Professor Leyland has extensive research program in the application of multilevel modelling to health data, and has given a number of training courses on this topic with colleagues in Europe. Further details can be found at professor Leyland's website:

http://www.msoc-mrc.gla.ac.uk/staff.php?staffID=AL

During his visit to Hobart Professor Leyland will also be a plenary speaker at the Joint Scientific Meetings of AEA/IEA-Western Pacific Region.

The workshop will begin with an introduction to multilevel data structures, fitting multilevel models and interpreting the results. The afternoon will be devoted to example applications of multilevel modelling in epidemiology, including some invited presentations from local speakers. There will be a session demonstrating software for multilevel modelling (although no "hands on" practical computing component), with plenty of time for discussion later in the day.

Although we will assume no previous experience in multilevel modelling, participants should be familiar with the use of statistical methods in epidemiology. Those with considerable knowledge and experience in multilevel modelling are also encouraged to attend since the planned program is diverse, and there will be ample opportunity to fraternise with local authorities on the subject!

The cost of the workshop will be $125 per person, which includes course materials and catering (lunch, morning and afternoon tea).  Registration will be through the AEA 2007 Conference Secretariat.
