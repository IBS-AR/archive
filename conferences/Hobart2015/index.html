The International Biometric Society Australasian Region Conference 2015

UPDATE: All talks made available by presenters are now online. Links to invited speakers talks are also just below, as well as to the slides used in the multiphase design course.

And many photos from all five days of the conference are up! Send more to the Webmaster, Hans Hockey, to upload to flickr or upload yourself to our new Facebook group, IBS-AR.

On behalf of the Australasian Region of the International Biometric Society (IBS-AR) we thank you for joining us for the biennial conference held in Hobart, Tasmania, on November 30 - December 3 (Monday - Thursday), 2015, at the Hadley's Orient Hotel. We were glad to share your experiences with using statistics in biosciences and learnt from others that do the same. Here is the exciting list of invited speakers, now with their talks available too:

  • Rosemary Bailey (University of St. Andrews) [talk pdf]

  • Adrian Bowman (University of Glasgow) [interactive talk]

  • Richard Emsley (University of Manchester) [talk pptx]

  • John Hinde (National University of Ireland) [talk pdf]

  • Jay Ver Hoef (NOAA Fairbanks Alaska) [talk pdf]

  • Katherine Lee (Murdock Children's Research Institute) [talk pptx]

  • Thomas Lumley (University of Auckland) [talk pdf]

  • Otso Ovaskainen (University of Helsinki) [talk pptx]

  • David Warton (University of NSW) [talk pptx]

The conference (November 30 - December 3 (Monday - Thursday)) was preceded by four parallel one-day courses on Sunday, November 29:

  • Rosemary Bailey, Chris Brien and Alison Smith - Multiphase experiments: from design to analysis [course pdfs]

  • Adrian Bowman - An introduction to flexible regression for environmental data

  • Richard Emsley - Causal inference in randomised trials

  • Otso Ovaskainen - Analyzing community data with joint species distribution models

We are happy that you attended and made the most of the vibrant conference in a beautiful location.
